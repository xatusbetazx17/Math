1) Use a property to complete: −2 · 5 = 5 · ____ (which property?)
Show steps
  1. Commutative property of multiplication: a·b = b·a.
  2. So (−2)·5 = 5·(−2). Answer: −2; property: commutative.
2) Perimeter of a rectangle: P = 2L + 2W. Solve for L.
Show steps
  1. Subtract 2W both sides: P − 2W = 2L.
  2. Divide both sides by 2: L = (P − 2W)/2.
3) A store gives a 9% cash discount. If the discount amount is $52, what was the bill before the discount? (nearest dollar)
Show steps
  1. Let total be T. Discount = 0.09T.
  2. 0.09T = 52 ⇒ T = 52 / 0.09 = 577.7… ≈ $578.
4) Run differential in 10 games: won 2 by 5; lost 3 by 3; lost 1 by 8; won 4 by 2. Net runs?
Show steps
  • +10 (2×5) − 9 (3×3) − 8 (1×8) + 8 (4×2) = +1.
5) Graph the linear equation y + 4 = 0.
Show steps
  • y + 4 = 0 ⇒ y = −4 is a constant‑y equation → horizontal line crossing the y‑axis at −4.
6) Slope through points (−9, 3) and (6, 8)
Show steps
  • m = (8−3)/(6−(−9)) = 5/15 = 1/3.
7) Solve and write interval: −7x ≤ 21
Show steps
  1. Divide by −7 (flip inequality): x ≥ −3.
  2. Interval: [−3, ∞).
8) Solve by elimination: x + 9y = 9, 7x − 5y = −5
Show steps
  1. From x + 9y = 9 ⇒ x = 9 − 9y.
  2. Substitute: 7(9−9y) − 5y = −5 ⇒ 63 − 68y = −5 ⇒ y = 1.
  3. x = 9 − 9(1) = 0. So (0, 1).
9) Solve: (x − 7)/4 = (x + 8)/7
Show steps
  • Cross‑multiply: 7(x−7)=4(x+8) ⇒ 7x−49=4x+32.
  • 3x = 81 ⇒ x = 27.
10) Solve: 6w + 9 = 10 − 7w − 6w
Show steps
  • Right side: −13w + 10. So 6w + 9 = −13w + 10.
  • 19w = 1 ⇒ w = 1/19.
11) Solve: −4(x + 5) = −(4x + 20)
Show steps
  • Both sides simplify to −4x − 20. Identity ⇒ true for all real x.
12) Solve: (2/5)x − (1/3)x = 2
Show steps
  • (6/15 − 5/15)x = (1/15)x = 2 ⇒ x = 30.
13) Solve: x² − x = 56
Show steps
  1. x² − x − 56 = 0 ⇒ factor: (x−8)(x+7)=0.
  2. Solutions: x = 8 or x = −7.
14) Line through (−7, 1) and (10, −5) in slope‑intercept form
Show steps
  1. m = (−5−1)/(10−(−7)) = −6/17.
  2. Use point (−7,1): y − 1 = (−6/17)(x + 7) ⇒ y = −(6/17)x − 25/17.
15) Factor completely: x² + 43x + 44
Show steps
  • Look for integers that multiply to 44 and add to 43 (1&44, 2&22, 4&11 → none add to 43).
  • Discriminant b² − 4ac = 43² − 176 = 1673 (not a perfect square) ⇒ not factorable over integers.
16) Evaluate (3x + 7)/(14 − 2y) for x = 9, y = 6.
Show steps
  • Numerator: 3·9 + 7 = 34; Denominator: 14 − 2·6 = 2; value = 34/2 = 17.
17) Evaluate 9x − 2y² for x = 3, y = −4.
Show steps
  • 9·3 − 2·(−4)² = 27 − 2·16 = 27 − 32 = −5.
18) Solve by substitution: x − 5y = 15, −4x − 4y = 12
Show steps
  1. x = 15 + 5y. Substitute: −4(15 + 5y) − 4y = 12.
  2. −60 − 20y − 4y = 12 ⇒ −24y = 72 ⇒ y = −3.
  3. x = 15 + 5(−3) = 0.
19) Divide: (y² + 18y + 81)/(y + 9)
Show steps
  • Factor numerator: y² + 18y + 81 = (y + 9)² ⇒ cancellation gives y + 9.
20) Complete the factoring: x² − 13x + 42 = (x − 7)( ____ )
Show steps
  • Find numbers that multiply to 42 and add to −13 ⇒ −7 and −6.
  • So (x − 7)(x − 6).
21) Write and simplify: “The product of −9 and 1, subtracted from −5.”
Show steps
  • “Subtracted from −5” ⇒ −5 − [ (−9)·1 ] = −5 − (−9) = 4.
22) Lowest terms: (16x + 20)/(20x + 25)
Show steps
  • Factor: 4(4x + 5)/[5(4x + 5)] = 4/5, provided x ≠ −5/4.
23) Lowest terms: (a² − 6a)/[(a + 9)(a − 6)]
Show steps
  • Factor numerator: a(a − 6); cancel (a − 6) ⇒ a/(a + 9) (domain: a ≠ −9, 6).
24) Simplify: (√(7t) + r)² (assume nonnegative variables)
Show steps
  • Use (A+B)² = A² + 2AB + B² with A=√(7t), B=r.
  • Gives 7t + 2r√(7t) + r².
25) Subtract: (7x + 30xy − 28y) − (14x − 29xy − 25y)
Show steps
  • Distribute minus: 7x+30xy−28y −14x+29xy+25y.
  • Combine like terms ⇒ (7−14)x = −7x; 30xy+29xy=59xy; −28y+25y=−3y.
